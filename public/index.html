Domestic Violence Happens Everywhere

Statistics
  • 603 million women live in countries where domestic violence is not yet considered a crime
  • 50% of women and girls aged 15-49 consider a husband justified in hitting his wife under certain circumstances
  • Women aged 15-44 are more at risk from rape and domestic violence than from cancer, motor accidents, war and malaria combined
  • Abusers harassing survivors via text messaging was reported by 96 percent of programs, while 86 percent reported that abusers harass victims through social media
  • Approximately 7 million women are raped and/or physically assaulted by a current or former intimate partner each year
  • Nationwide, an average of 3 women are killed by a current or former intimate partner every day

We can Help

Domestic violence happens around the world. People suffer from abuse and are unaware of how to handle the situation. Besides the victim, the people most concerned are the friends and family members of the person who is getting abused. Often people who are getting abused will not reach out for help or are not aware that they are victims of domestic violence. This is where the friends and family members come in. Through SMS, Help140 educates the friends and family members by offering ways to start the conversation about the victim's abuse to attempt to resolve the issues at hand by providing guidance and counseling to fight the abuse.

Physical Abuse
Symptoms of physical abuse are bruising, broken bones, pain, cuts or scars, marks left by a gag, frequent sore throats, sprains, and/or imprint injuries.
Psychological Abuse
Symptoms of psychological abuse are humiliation, having a dominant power over the victim, the victim is blamed for everything- especially things that are not true, and/or the victim is threatened.

Emotional Abuse
Symptoms of emotional abuse are being yelled at or cursed at (emotional bullying), humiliation, isolation, and/or threats and intimidation.
Sexual Abuse
Symptoms of sexual abuse are fear responses at the reminder of the assaulter's name, depression, anger, anxiety, loss of interest in sex, and/or disruptions in close relationships.
Economical Abuse
Symptoms of economical abuse are the assaulter prevents the victim from getting a job, victim's money is monitored, assaulter takes victim's money, and/or the victim has no control over financial decisions.
